# GrainBids two-minute demo script

**Audience:** farmer beta user, elevator owner, grain merchandiser, or commercial buyer.

## Before the demo

- Use public, synthetic, anonymized, or explicitly authorized data only.
- Confirm the visible date, source timestamp, currency, unit, delivery period, and region.
- Do not use employer-confidential bids, customer names, contracts, margins, or private screenshots.
- If the data is stale or incomplete, show the warning instead of hiding it.

## 0:00–0:20 — Frame the problem

“Grain prices are not one number. The useful comparison depends on buyer, location, delivery period, unit, currency, futures context, and freight. GrainBids puts that context in one place. It is market information, not a trading instruction or guaranteed executable quote.”

## 0:20–0:55 — Show the market view

Open the bids view and select one commodity, one location group, and one delivery period.

Say: “This is the posted market view. The source and timing stay visible so a user can verify the quote. The table is designed for comparison, not for pretending that every row is identical.”

For a farmer, ask: “What buyer or delivery window would you normally compare here?”

For a merchandiser, ask: “Which locations, competing buyers, or recurring lanes would your team want beside this view?”

## 0:55–1:20 — Show movement and context

Open the relevant signal, chart, or historical view if available.

Say: “A current bid without change context is hard to interpret. The useful question is what moved, over which comparable period, and whether the movement is cash, basis, futures, FX, or a data-quality issue.”

Do not describe a movement as a recommendation. State the observed comparison and its limitations.

## 1:20–1:45 — Show the commercial output

Show the weekly report preview, source-health panel, or a synthetic freight comparison.

Say: “For a commercial team, GrainBids can turn this into a reviewable brief: strongest observations, material changes, stale or missing sources, and freight assumptions. The customer verifies executable bids and keeps the pricing decision.”

## 1:45–2:00 — Route to the right next step

For farmers: “The free beta is for learning which buyers, locations, alerts, and charts make this useful in real sale decisions. The next step is one normal comparison task and brief feedback.”

For elevators or merchandisers: “If this manual comparison is recurring, the smallest paid test is a CAD $750 snapshot using existing verified coverage. A CAD $2,500 pilot is only appropriate after we confirm a daily workflow, source permission, buyer authority, and a measurable 14-day outcome.”

## Demo close questions

- What would you have checked before opening this view?
- Which item is missing or unclear?
- What decision would this support, and how often does it recur?
- Would a snapshot, beta access, or no next step make sense?
