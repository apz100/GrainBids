# GrainBids Service Offer

## Commercial objective

Sell a small number of grain businesses a managed pricing-intelligence service before building a broad self-serve product.

The operating rule is **pain → offer → payment → build**:

1. Confirm an expensive, recurring pricing or market-monitoring problem.
2. Sell the smallest fixed-scope deliverable that proves value.
3. Collect payment before creating customer-specific automation.
4. Build only the reusable parts required to deliver paid work.

GrainBids is not generic elevator consulting and is not a replacement for a merchandiser. It turns scattered public and customer-authorized grain pricing into a decision-ready view of cash bids, basis, delivery periods, freight-adjusted alternatives, and material market changes.

## Core positioning

> GrainBids shows commercial grain teams who is paying what, where basis moved, which destinations remain competitive after freight, and which changes deserve attention—without requiring staff to check dozens of websites and spreadsheets manually.

The initial product is a managed commercial-intelligence service supported by software. Customers buy a useful result, faster decisions, and reduced monitoring work—not access to unfinished technology.

## Ideal customer profile priority

### Priority 1 — Independent elevators, grain brokers, feed mills, and processors

Best fit:

- One to ten locations in Ontario or an adjacent region.
- An owner, general manager, merchandiser, grain buyer, or procurement manager controls the purchase.
- Staff manually check competitor websites, phone bids, email sheets, futures, and freight.
- A missed bid move, weak basis, or poorly selected destination can cost more than the service fee.
- The business can approve a $750 snapshot or $2,500 pilot without enterprise procurement.

This is the first target because the pain is concentrated, commercially important, and directly connected to existing GrainBids proof.

### Priority 2 — Regional multi-location grain buyers and agricultural input businesses

Best fit:

- Five to 30 locations or several procurement territories.
- Market monitoring and price reporting vary by location.
- Commercial leadership needs standardized reports, exceptions, source health, and regional comparisons.

These accounts can support larger contracts, but generally require more validation, permissions, and integration work.

### Priority 3 — Large commercial farms and farm-management groups

Best fit:

- Significant annual marketable production.
- Sells to several destinations and actively compares delivery windows, basis, and freight.
- Values independent local-market intelligence and a structured marketing review.

Use this segment selectively. Small individual farms are not the lead market because their willingness to pay is generally lower than that of commercial buyers.

## Product ladder

### Package 1 — Competitive Market Snapshot

**Price:** CAD $750 one time, paid in advance.

**Delivery:** Within 48 hours when feasible and no later than three business days after payment and receipt of required inputs.

**Purpose:** Give a prospect a fast, useful answer using data GrainBids already has and can verify. This is the lowest-friction paid entry point; it is not a custom software project.

**Exact deliverables:**

- One defined region.
- Up to two commodities.
- Analysis of existing verified public GrainBids data from up to eight available sources.
- A normalized cash-bid table with location, delivery period, units, source timestamp, and confidence notes.
- Basis comparisons where futures month, currency, units, and delivery period can be matched defensibly.
- One simple freight-adjusted destination comparison using up to three customer-supplied freight assumptions.
- Three to five commercially relevant observations: strongest bids, notable spread or basis differences, stale/missing sources, and important data limitations.
- One polished PDF or spreadsheet deliverable.
- One 30-minute findings call.

**Customer inputs required:**

- Region and commodities.
- Up to three recurring destinations or buyers.
- Freight rates or costs if a delivered-value comparison is requested.
- Any delivery-window constraint that materially changes the comparison.

**Strict boundaries:**

- Existing verified public data only; no new scraper, source integration, historical backfill, or custom automation.
- No daily monitoring, alerts, portal access, or custom dashboard.
- No automated trading, contracting, or customer communication.
- No unsupported recommendation to buy or sell grain.
- The customer verifies executable bids with each counterparty.

**Conversion:** Credit the CAD $750 snapshot fee against Package 2 if the customer approves and pays for the pilot within seven calendar days of the findings call.

### Package 2 — Competitive Bid Intelligence Pilot

**Price:** CAD $2,500 paid in advance for a 14-day engagement.

**Purpose:** Prove that GrainBids can save staff time and reveal commercially useful pricing differences in one commodity/region before either party commits to a recurring service.

**Exact deliverables:**

- One 45-minute kickoff with the commercial decision-maker.
- Monitoring of up to 12 agreed public or customer-authorized bid sources.
- Coverage of up to two commodities and one defined region.
- One decision-ready morning email each business day for 10 business days.
- Normalized cash prices, basis where defensibly calculable, delivery periods, locations, source timestamp, and confidence flags.
- Material-change callouts: new high/low, meaningful basis or cash-price movement, missing/stale source, and newly attractive delivery alternative.
- One freight-adjusted destination comparison using customer-supplied assumptions.
- One closing 45-minute findings review.
- A pilot scorecard showing sources monitored, manual hours avoided, material changes found, data gaps, and recommended production scope.

**Boundaries:**

- Only the agreed source, commodity, region, and report scope is included.
- Source failures are disclosed rather than silently estimated.
- Customer-specific assumptions and confidential information remain segregated from public GrainBids outputs.
- Customer retains every pricing and execution decision.

**Conversion:** Credit the CAD $2,500 pilot fee against Package 3 onboarding if the customer starts the managed desk within 14 calendar days of the closing review.

### Package 3 — Managed Grain Market Desk

**Price:** CAD $2,000 onboarding plus CAD $2,500 per month, billed monthly in advance. Three-month initial term.

**Purpose:** Replace repetitive bid checking and spreadsheet assembly with a dependable daily commercial-intelligence workflow.

**Exact deliverables:**

- Up to 25 agreed public, licensed, or customer-authorized sources.
- Up to four commodities across one region.
- A business-day morning market brief delivered to up to five recipients.
- Normalized cash-bid and basis tables by location and delivery window.
- Customer-defined freight assumptions and delivered-value comparisons for up to five recurring lanes.
- Alerts for material bid changes, stale/missing sources, and agreed thresholds.
- Weekly basis and competitive-position summary.
- One monthly 45-minute commercial review.
- Source-health report and transparent confidence labels.
- One approved report-template adjustment per month.
- Downloadable CSV or XLSX extract of the covered customer data.

**Service level:** The morning brief is targeted for an agreed time on business days. Stale or unavailable sources are clearly marked.

**Not included:** ERP integration, custom portals, historical backfills, new proprietary data licences, or unlimited analysis. These require separate written scope and payment.

## Post-validation custom scope

A multi-region pricing-intelligence system is available only after GrainBids has completed a paid pilot or managed engagement and validated the customer’s workflow.

Possible scope includes more than 25 sources, several regions, customer-specific FX and freight logic, role-based outputs, integrations, historical trends, and controlled source promotion or quarantine. Pricing starts only after discovery, data-rights review, and a paid design phase. Do not use this as the initial offer or pre-build it for a prospect.

## Payment and sales terms

- Package 1 is the default entry when a prospect wants proof quickly or is not ready for daily monitoring.
- Package 2 is the default entry when recurring monitoring pain and purchase authority are already confirmed.
- No unpaid custom proof of concept.
- Work starts after cleared payment and a signed scope.
- Prices are in CAD and exclude applicable tax and third-party data-licensing fees.
- Additional sources, commodities, regions, integrations, or reports require a change order.
- Either party may decline conversion after a snapshot or pilot.
- Package 3 renews monthly after its initial term unless terminated with 30 days’ notice.
- A short service agreement must cover data provenance, confidentiality, limitations, permitted users, customer verification, and no execution authority.

## Proof and demo assets

Build only enough to sell and deliver the paid ladder:

1. **An anonymized morning brief:** A polished email or PDF showing cash bids, basis, timestamps, confidence, and three material changes.
2. **A competitive-bid table:** Normalized Eastern Ontario corn and soybean examples with location and delivery-period filters.
3. **A freight-adjusted comparison:** Three destinations with explicit distance/rate assumptions and netback ranking.
4. **A seven-day basis-change chart:** Proof that GrainBids preserves changes rather than displaying only today’s number.
5. **A source-health panel:** Last successful collection, stale data, failure disclosure, and candidate/pilot/active/quarantined status.
6. **A two-minute narrated demo:** The problem, daily brief, one useful market change, source confidence, and paid next step.
7. **A one-page scope for each entry offer:** Deliverables, timetable, customer inputs, price, boundaries, signature, and payment link.
8. **A pilot scorecard:** Hours saved, sources covered, important changes caught, decisions supported, and production recommendation.

Every demo must use public, synthetic, anonymized, or explicitly authorized data. Never expose an employer’s confidential bids, customer information, margins, contracts, or internal workflows.

## Discovery questions

Use a 20-minute commercial call to ask:

1. Who currently checks competitor bids, futures, basis, and destination values?
2. Which commodities, delivery periods, and territories matter most each morning?
3. How many websites, emails, phone calls, and spreadsheets are involved?
4. How much staff time does the process consume in a normal week?
5. What happens commercially when a major bid change is caught late or missed?
6. Which comparisons are hardest: cash price, basis, freight-adjusted netback, FX, or delivery timing?
7. Which destinations and freight assumptions recur often enough to standardize?
8. Which sources are public, licensed, or available with customer authorization?
9. How does the team determine whether a number is current and trustworthy?
10. What change should trigger an alert rather than wait for the next report?
11. Who consumes the output, and what format will they actually open?
12. Would a one-time answer or 10 business days of monitoring better prove value?
13. What would make that deliverable unquestionably worth CAD $750 or $2,500?
14. Who approves the purchase, and can that person attend the findings review?

Do not design a solution during discovery. Confirm the pain, economic importance, data access, buyer, success measure, and appropriate paid next step.

## Common objections and responses

### “We already check the bids ourselves.”

> That is the workflow GrainBids is designed to improve. The snapshot gives you a paid, fixed-scope way to judge the output before committing to monitoring. The pilot then measures time saved and useful changes caught.

### “The public bids aren’t always executable.”

> Correct. GrainBids preserves source and timestamp, flags stale or uncertain data, and requires your team to verify an executable quote. It improves monitoring; it does not represent every posted indication as a firm contract.

### “We can build this in Excel.”

> You probably can. The value is the collection, normalization, failure handling, freight logic, and decision-ready result—not the existence of a spreadsheet.

### “We already use DTN, Barchart, or another platform.”

> GrainBids can complement licensed market data by organizing the local commercial view around your competitors, destinations, freight assumptions, and exceptions. We proceed only if the snapshot identifies value your current system does not provide.

### “Your coverage is only Eastern Ontario.”

> Eastern Ontario is the proven starting dataset, not the product boundary. A snapshot uses already verified coverage. New regional sources are considered only in a paid pilot and activated gradually after reliability and permission checks.

### “What if a scraper breaks?”

> Source health and failure disclosure are part of the deliverable. A failed source is marked stale or unavailable; GrainBids does not silently fabricate a bid. Repeated failures are quarantined.

### “Why should we pay before seeing custom work?”

> The CAD $750 snapshot is itself a complete deliverable using existing verified data. Custom collection or automation is excluded. If it proves useful, the full fee can be credited toward the 14-day pilot.

### “Can you guarantee better trading results?”

> No. GrainBids guarantees the agreed analysis and monitoring process, not market outcomes. Your authorized staff retain all pricing and execution decisions.

### “Can you customize everything before we decide?”

> The snapshot and pilot have fixed scopes. We configure only the inputs needed for the purchased deliverable. Additional custom work begins after a written scope and payment are approved.

## What not to build yet

- A nationwide public marketplace before a customer pays for one regional workflow.
- A farmer social network, contracting exchange, or brokerage function.
- An elevator ERP, accounting system, inventory platform, or generic CRM.
- Automated trade execution or contract acceptance.
- A polished multi-tenant self-serve SaaS onboarding system.
- Native mobile applications.
- Custom dashboards or new scrapers for unpaid prospects.
- Hundreds of unverified sources activated at once.
- Historical backfills for every commodity and geography.
- AI-generated market commentary without source-linked facts and human review.
- Automated mass email, social posting, or outbound spam.
- Paid API commitments before a signed customer scope requires them.
- Complex subscription tiers aimed at small farms.
- A large regional enterprise system before a paid engagement validates the requirements.
- Features designed only to impress rather than reduce measured commercial pain.
- Any workflow using an employer’s confidential data, customer information, internal margins, or proprietary methods without explicit authorization.

## Build priority after payment

For a snapshot, do not build software. Use verified existing data and deliver the analysis.

For a paid pilot, build only the smallest reusable delivery path required by the signed scope:

1. Activate and validate the customer-approved source set.
2. Normalize location, commodity, units, delivery period, basis, timestamp, and confidence.
3. Apply customer-approved freight and comparison rules.
4. Generate the daily brief with a human approval step.
5. Log failures, corrections, and customer feedback.
6. Automate only repeated delivery work proven valuable during the pilot.

The milestone is not more GrainBids features. It is a paid snapshot, a paid pilot, a documented result, a testimonial or anonymized case study, and a repeatable path to the next customer.
