# GrainBids for elevators and merchandisers

## The problem

Commercial teams often assemble a local market view from buyer websites, emails, phone bids, futures screens, freight assumptions, and spreadsheets. The difficult part is not finding one number. It is knowing which numbers are comparable, current, and commercially relevant.

## The outcome

GrainBids turns scattered public and customer-authorized pricing into a source-attributed view of:

- who is posting what by location and delivery period;
- where cash price or basis moved;
- which sources are stale, missing, or inconsistent;
- how freight changes a destination comparison;
- which changes deserve a commercial team's attention.

It supports a merchandiser's work. It does not replace the merchandiser, execute trades, or treat a posted indication as a firm bid.

## Best first use cases

1. **Competitive bid snapshot** — a fixed view of up to two commodities and one region.
2. **Daily bid-intelligence pilot** — 10 business days of decision-ready monitoring.
3. **Freight/netback comparison** — customer-supplied lanes and assumptions made explicit.
4. **Content/report automation** — fact-first market drafts with source, timestamp, and QA lineage.

## What a first snapshot requires

- one region and up to two commodities;
- the buyer or destination comparisons that matter;
- existing verified public GrainBids coverage;
- up to three customer-supplied freight assumptions if delivered value is requested;
- the person who will review the findings.

## How value is measured

The first engagement measures process value, not promised trading profit:

- hours of repetitive checking avoided;
- material bid or source changes surfaced;
- sources and locations covered;
- incompatible or stale observations disclosed;
- decisions or meetings supported by the output.

## Evidence and limits

Every output should disclose source, timestamp, location, commodity, unit, currency, delivery period, futures month where applicable, and freight treatment. Basis comparisons require compatible futures, currency, unit, and delivery timing. Customer teams verify executable bids and retain every pricing decision.
