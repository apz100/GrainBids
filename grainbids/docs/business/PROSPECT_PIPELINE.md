# GrainBids initial B2B prospect pipeline

**Research date:** 2026-07-14  
**Scope:** Public information only. No prospect has been contacted, no private contact data is included, and no buying workflow has been represented as confirmed without discovery.

## How to use this pipeline

This is an account list, not a mailing list. The observable facts below come from public company pages, public cash-bid pages, or GrainBids' approved public-source registry. Every pain statement is explicitly a **hypothesis** to test in discovery.

Prioritize accounts using three practical tiers:

- **Tier A:** Regional, commercially relevant, likely reachable decision-maker, and a strong match to existing Ontario proof. Research these first.
- **Tier B:** Strong use case but larger organization, longer sales cycle, or weaker current GrainBids coverage.
- **Tier C:** Geographic expansion or strategic account. Keep as a learning target until the Ontario offer converts.

Offer selection follows the existing product ladder:

- **CAD $750 snapshot:** default when the pain, data access, or authority is not yet confirmed.
- **CAD $2,500 pilot:** use only where public evidence already shows recurring multi-location/processing procurement and discovery confirms a daily monitoring problem.

### Verification labels

- **V1:** Official site and a relevant public cash-bid/source page were identified.
- **V2:** Official site and segment/location were identified; the exact bid/procurement workflow still needs verification.
- **V3:** Official URL exists in GrainBids' approved candidate registry, but the current bid path or company details need rechecking before use.

## Research conclusions and guardrails

1. **Ontario should produce the first sale.** Accounts 1-10 align best with current data, domain credibility, and a short paid-snapshot path.
2. **Processors need a different story from elevators.** Sell delivered procurement visibility to feed/ethanol/soy processors; sell competitive position and exception reporting to elevators and merchants.
3. **Public bids do not prove pain or authority.** They prove that a sample may be possible. Discovery must confirm who performs the work, time spent, commercial consequences, data permissions, and purchase authority.
4. **Do not bulk-scrape these accounts.** US candidates remain manual/inactive until source rights, technical reliability, units, and a commercial reason are reviewed.
5. **Do not use employer-confidential knowledge.** Proof should use public, synthetic, anonymized, or explicitly authorized information only.
6. **Do not outreach autonomously.** Before any contact, verify the account, role, offer angle, and approved message; recipient resolution and sending require explicit authorization.
