# GrainBids farmer beta program

**Purpose:** recruit a small, useful first group of Ontario farmers to test whether GrainBids helps them compare local corn, soybean, and wheat bids before building farmer-facing features beyond the current market view.

## The promise

GrainBids gives farmers one place to compare posted local grain bids, delivery periods, locations, basis context, and historical movement. It is a market-information tool—not a broker, buyer, trading adviser, or guarantee that a posted bid is executable.

## Beta audience

Recruit 10–25 Ontario farmers across Eastern Ontario first. Include a mix of:

- corn, soybean, and wheat growers;
- farms selling into more than one nearby buyer or destination;
- farms that currently compare bids manually through websites, phone calls, texts, or spreadsheets;
- both frequent GrainBids users and people who will test the product critically.

Do not require a specific acreage, production system, or exact farmer profile. The beta is for broad farmer usefulness, with enough commercial variation to expose coverage gaps.

## What beta users receive

- free access to the current GrainBids market view during the beta;
- a short weekly question or feedback request, not an automatic sales sequence;
- priority consideration for nearby-location comparisons, alerts, and historical charts;
- a transparent changelog when a beta suggestion changes the product.

No farmer is promised a price improvement, a trading outcome, a complete Ontario market, or preferential access to a buyer.

## What GrainBids needs from them

The minimum ask is 15 minutes during the first two weeks:

1. try one normal bid-comparison task;
2. identify one missing buyer, location, commodity, delivery period, or unit;
3. describe what they would check before making a sale decision;
4. rate whether the output was understandable and useful;
5. optionally provide a public bid URL or a screenshot of a bid they are already allowed to share.

Customer or employer-confidential quotes, contracts, farm records, and personal financial information are not required for the beta.

## Beta workflow

| Stage | User action | GrainBids evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Join | Consent-tracked signup with farmer audience and beta-interest source | Subscriber record; no outreach automation |
| First use | Compare one commodity, location, and delivery period | Task completion note |
| Feedback | Report one useful result and one confusing or missing item | Feedback record |
| Review | GrainBids groups requests by frequency and commercial value | Prioritized backlog entry |
| Release | Only validated, safe improvements are shipped | Changelog and test evidence |

## Feedback questions

Use five questions, one at a time where possible:

1. What were you trying to decide when you opened GrainBids?
2. Which buyer, location, commodity, delivery period, or unit were you looking for?
3. What did you compare before GrainBids, and what was still missing afterward?
4. Would an alert, nearby-buyer comparison, historical chart, or clearer delivery filter be most useful next?
5. Would you use this weekly during marketing decisions, and what would make you return?

Record the user's words separately from the team's interpretation. Do not convert interest into a revenue claim.

## Beta success criteria

The beta is useful if, within the first 30 days:

- at least 10 farmers complete one real comparison task;
- at least 6 return for a second session or request a recurring view;
- at least 5 identify the same high-value improvement;
- source or location gaps are recorded with enough detail to reproduce them;
- no material privacy, consent, or misleading-price issue is reported.

These are learning thresholds, not promises to participants. If the thresholds fail, improve the product or narrow the claim before adding features.

## Guardrails

- Use only public, synthetic, anonymized, or explicitly authorized data.
- Keep consent and unsubscribe behavior intact.
- Label posted indications with source, timestamp, currency, unit, delivery period, and confidence where available.
- Never present a posted bid as an executable offer without counterparty confirmation.
- Never recommend that a farmer buy, sell, hedge, or wait based solely on GrainBids.
- Do not sell farmer data or disclose individual usage to grain businesses.
- Do not add an automated email sequence until content, consent, sender configuration, and unsubscribe handling are separately approved.
