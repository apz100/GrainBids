# GrainBids master execution plan

## North star

Turn GrainBids into a low-overhead grain-intelligence business that produces near-term service revenue and compounds into reusable data products, content, and software.

GrainBids is not currently a bet on owning or operating an elevator. It is the data, analysis, reporting, and automation system behind services sold to grain businesses.

## Business sequence

1. Use existing bid data and software to produce credible demonstrations and paid custom outputs.
2. Sell a narrow outcome before expanding the application.
3. Standardize repeated client work into templates and automated workflows.
4. Turn validated recurring workflows into subscriptions or software features.
5. Expand geography source-by-source with quality and load controls.

## Three active tracks

### 1. Revenue

Build productized offers for elevators, merchandisers, brokers, feed mills, processors, agricultural service firms, and larger farms. Prioritize competitive-bid intelligence, market reporting, data cleanup, reporting automation, and custom monitoring.

### 2. Product and data

Make GrainBids a reliable proof and delivery engine: controlled source onboarding, provenance, QA, regional configuration, customer-ready reports, and measurable data freshness. Build only the software needed for a demo, delivery, or validated recurring service.

### 3. Content and operations

Convert verified data into draft newsletters, website insights, charts, social posts, and prospect-specific samples. Maintain the backlog, reusable instructions, templates, and approval gates that let agents continue safely.

## Current platform state

- Email delivery remains disabled by default.
- Draft PR #3 adds controlled regional-source candidates, explicit pilot promotion, and quarantine controls.

- The existing Ontario ingestion stack and historical data provide the initial proof base.

## Ninety-day outcomes

- One offer that can be explained in one sentence and demonstrated with GrainBids.
- Three customer-ready sample deliverables for distinct B2B use cases.
- First paid pilot or written commitment before major new SaaS scope.
- A repeatable daily/weekly draft-content workflow with evidence and approval gates.
- A source-onboarding workflow that can validate one region without bulk activation.
- A small prospect pipeline with tracked conversations, objections, and next actions.

## Decision rules

Score proposed work from 1-5 on:

- revenue proximity;
- customer proof value;
- recurring manual effort removed;
- reuse across customers or regions;
- implementation speed;
- operational and reputational risk, scored in reverse.

Prefer work with high revenue/proof/reuse and low time/risk. If a task does not improve a sale, delivery, data reliability, or repeatability, pause it.

## Standing safety gates

Drafting, research, local tests, reversible code, skills, documentation, and draft pull requests may proceed autonomously.

Require explicit authorization for live outreach, email/social sending, paid tools, customer commitments, activating new scraping, destructive changes, and merges not already authorized by the current request.

## Execution cadence

At every work cycle:

1. Reconcile the backlog against GitHub and completed artifacts.
2. Keep one task active in each of the three tracks.
3. Produce a reviewable artifact, test result, or business deliverable per active task.
4. Record evidence and the next action.
5. Start the next safe queued task without waiting for a new prompt.
