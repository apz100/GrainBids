# GrainBids sales discovery kit

**Purpose:** Qualify a commercial grain-monitoring problem before offering custom work. This kit supports a 20-minute conversation and an internal go/no-go decision. It is not outreach copy, a promise of coverage, or permission to contact anyone.

## Required preparation

Before a conversation:

1. Read the account's section in `FIRST_10_ACCOUNT_BRIEFS.md`.
2. Separate verified public facts from pain hypotheses and unknowns.
3. Select one relevant proof angle, but do not build a custom sample.
4. Record the role expected to own the workflow; do not guess a named person.
5. Keep the CAD $750 snapshot as the default paid step. Treat the CAD $2,500 pilot as conditional.

## Opening framing

> GrainBids is a data-supported service for organizing verified public and customer-authorized grain pricing into a clearer commercial view. I do not yet know whether it fits your workflow. I would like to understand how you currently compare bids, basis, delivery periods, and freight-adjusted alternatives; which data you may authorize; and what a useful result would need to change. Public prices remain source-attributed indications, and your team would verify any executable quote and retain every pricing decision.

Do not say that GrainBids saves time, catches missed moves, has complete coverage, improves margins, or supports a specific account until evidence establishes it. Ask; do not diagnose aloud from a public website.

## Twenty-minute call guide

| Time | Objective | Required output |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 minutes | Set the evidence and decision boundaries | Agreement that this is workflow discovery, not trading advice or a coverage promise |
| 2–6 minutes | Map the present workflow | Owner role, frequency, sources, commodities, region, delivery periods, and output |
| 6–10 minutes | Establish pain and consequence | Manual effort, failure modes, commercial importance, and current workaround |
| 10–14 minutes | Establish data feasibility and permission | Public/licensed/authorized sources, freight inputs, restrictions, and gaps |
| 14–17 minutes | Establish authority and timing | Buyer role, budget path, urgency, and other required approvers |
| 17–20 minutes | Define success and route | No-fit, bounded free follow-up, $750 snapshot, or conditional $2,500 pilot |

Do not design software during the call. Restate the problem, unknowns, evidence needed, and one next step.

## Question bank

Use the account brief's three tailored questions first, then fill material gaps from this bank. A 20-minute call should not become an interrogation.

### Workflow and pain

- Who owns competitor-bid, basis, destination, or procurement-price monitoring today?
- Which region, facilities, commodities, grades, delivery periods, and destinations matter?
- How often is the view rebuilt, and from which websites, emails, calls, licences, or spreadsheets?
- Which part takes the most work: collection, normalization, basis matching, freight, source checking, or presentation?
- What goes wrong now—stale sources, incompatible terms, missed changes, repeated checking, or unclear delivered value?
- How much staff time does the process consume in a normal week? Mark the answer as reported, not verified.
- What commercial decision uses the output, and what happens when the view is late or incomplete?

### Value and success

- Is the immediate need a one-time answer, periodic review, or business-day monitoring?
- Which one or two changes would deserve attention rather than another full market table?
- What observable result would make a paid deliverable worthwhile?
- For a recurring pilot, can success be measured through hours avoided, source coverage, material exceptions, user adoption, or another agreed process measure?
- What current report or decision would the output replace or improve?

Do not use speculative margin gains or trading performance as the success measure.

### Authority, budget, and timing

- Who owns the problem, who approves payment, and who must accept the output?
- Can the buying authority approve CAD $750 paid in advance for a fixed snapshot?
- If daily monitoring is proven relevant, can the organization approve CAD $2,500 paid in advance for a 14-day pilot?
- Is there a decision date, seasonal window, recurring meeting, or operational event creating real urgency?
- What else must be reviewed—procurement, IT, legal, data licensing, or management?
- Can the commercial decision-maker attend the findings review?

### Data and permission

- Which proposed sources are public, licensed, customer-owned, or third-party confidential?
- For each non-public source, who may authorize access and the specific permitted use?
- Do licences allow automated retrieval, storage, normalization, derived analysis, and sharing with intended recipients?
- Are credentials, access controls, paywalls, anti-bot measures, or contractual restrictions involved?
- What currency, unit, grade, futures month, delivery period, timestamp, and location identity are required?
- Who supplies and validates freight, FX, quality, handling, or other customer-specific assumptions?
- What data must remain segregated from public GrainBids outputs, and how long may it be retained?
- Are historical observations already authorized and comparable, or would the work start prospectively?

“The customer can see it” is not proof that GrainBids may collect, store, or process it.

## Qualification scorecard

Score each dimension from 0 to 2 using call evidence. Unknown is 0, not 1.

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recurring pain | No defined workflow or need | Occasional/manual inconvenience | Repeated, owned workflow with a concrete failure mode |
| Commercial importance | No consequence identified | Qualitative importance only | Specific decision, time burden, or process consequence identified |
| Scope fit | Broad, custom, or outside GrainBids | Scope needs narrowing | One region, up to two commodities, and a clear output |
| Data readiness | Sources or terms unknown/unusable | Some public data; material gaps remain | Required public or authorized inputs are identified and plausibly usable |
| Authority and funding | No buyer or payment path | Influencer can reach buyer | Buyer and paid-in-advance path are known |
| Timing | No reason to act | General interest | Defined operational window or decision date |
| Measurable success | “Useful” only | One qualitative outcome | Observable process measures and reviewer are agreed |

**Total:** ____ / 14
**Hard-stop present:** Yes / No
**Required dimensions for pilot all score 2:** recurring pain / data readiness / authority and funding / measurable success.

The score informs judgment; it does not override permissions, source feasibility, capacity, or the fixed offer boundaries.

## Red flags and stop rules

Stop or decline when any of the following applies:

- The prospect wants guaranteed trading, pricing, margin, procurement, or market outcomes.
- The work requires representing a public indication as an executable bid without counterparty confirmation.
- The prospect requests trading execution, contract authority, or automated buy/sell decisions.
- Necessary data is private, employer-confidential, improperly obtained, or lacks documented authorization.
- A source requires bypassing authentication, access controls, rate limits, robots restrictions, or contractual limits.
- Licensed data rights do not cover the intended retrieval, storage, transformation, or distribution.
- Incompatible grades, units, currencies, futures months, timestamps, or delivery periods cannot be reconciled defensibly.
- The requested region or source set cannot be validated within the fixed paid scope.
- The buyer expects a free custom proof, new scraper, historical backfill, portal, integration, or unlimited revisions.
- No owner, decision, consequence, budget path, or timing can be identified after discovery.
- The work would expose customer-authorized information in public GrainBids content.
- The account asks GrainBids to duplicate a satisfactory internal system without a narrow unmet need.

Record the reason plainly. Do not build features to rescue an unqualified opportunity.

## Routing decision

Hard stops override score thresholds.

### No fit / close

Use when the score is 0–4, a hard stop cannot be resolved, the problem is outside GrainBids, or the existing workflow is satisfactory.

**Action:** Record the reason and close. Do not schedule speculative work.

### Bounded free follow-up

Use when the score is 5–7 and exactly one bounded factual or qualification gap could change the route.

Allowed follow-up is limited to one existing standard asset, one public-source verification, or one clarification question requiring no more than 30 minutes. It cannot include custom analysis, live prices, a new collection path, historical research, freight modelling, a proposal, or account-specific software.

**Action:** Record the single question, responsible party, deadline, and decision it unlocks. If it remains unresolved, close.

### CAD $750 Competitive Market Snapshot

Use when the score is at least 8 and all of these are true:

- one region and up to two commodities answer a defined commercial question;
- existing verified public GrainBids data can support the work;
- up to eight available sources are sufficient;
- any freight comparison uses no more than three customer-supplied, documented assumptions;
- the buyer accepts a fixed deliverable, no custom collection or automation, signed scope, and payment in advance;
- source and bid limitations can be disclosed without undermining the question.

**Action:** Prepare a fixed scope only after verifying source feasibility. Delivery is within 48 hours when feasible and no later than three business days after cleared payment and required inputs.

### Conditional CAD $2,500 Competitive Bid Intelligence Pilot

Use only when the score is at least 11, no hard stop exists, and recurring pain, data readiness, authority/funding, and measurable success each score 2. Also require:

- one region, up to two commodities, and up to 12 agreed public or customer-authorized sources;
- a real business-day monitoring need that a one-time snapshot cannot test;
- ten business-day reporting, one freight comparison, and the existing pilot boundaries are accepted;
- a named internal role—not necessarily a named person in this artifact—owns inputs and reviews outputs;
- source feasibility and permission are documented before inclusion;
- success measures, kickoff, closing review, signed scope, and payment in advance are accepted.

**Action:** Complete the paid-pilot package and source feasibility review. Do not promise or activate an unvalidated source merely to close the pilot. If one prerequisite fails, route to snapshot, bounded follow-up, or no fit.

## Customer data-permission checklist

Complete one record per non-public or licensed source before access. Public sources still require a source-feasibility record.

### Engagement-level controls

- [ ] Legal customer entity and authorized approving role are recorded.
- [ ] Region, commodities, facilities, delivery periods, intended recipients, and purpose are defined.
- [ ] Public, licensed, customer-owned, and third-party-confidential inputs are separated.
- [ ] Public GrainBids data and customer-confidential outputs are segregated.
- [ ] Confidentiality, permitted users, retention, deletion, and incident contacts are in the signed scope.
- [ ] Customer retains pricing decisions and verifies executable bids with counterparties.
- [ ] No credentials or sensitive values are stored in CRM notes or business documentation.

Store business information needed to manage the opportunity; avoid unnecessary personal or sensitive data.

| Opportunity | Problem statement, current workflow, desired output, frequency, commodities, delivery periods, competing destinations |

## Review gate before a paid scope

Confirm all five statements:

1. The problem and scope were stated by the prospect, not inferred from the account brief.
2. The proposed data is verified public data or has documented customer authorization.
3. The offer matches the score and every routing prerequisite.
4. The scope states units, currency, grade, delivery, futures, freight, source, timestamp, confidence, and bid-verification limits where relevant.
5. No custom work starts before signed scope, cleared payment, and required inputs.
