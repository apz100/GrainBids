# GrainBids first-ten account briefs

**Prepared:** 2026-07-14
**Evidence boundary:** Public information already recorded in `PROSPECT_PIPELINE.md` and source behavior already represented in the GrainBids repository. No live bid, price, contact, or private-company data was added. Every pain statement is a hypothesis for discovery, not a claim about the account.

## How to use these briefs

- Verify the checklist before creating any account-specific sample or outreach draft.
- Use roles only until named-recipient research and outreach are explicitly authorized.
- Never imply that a public bid is executable; show source time, delivery period, currency, unit, futures month, and confidence.
- Default to the CAD $750 snapshot. Offer the CAD $2,500 pilot only after discovery confirms recurring daily work, source permission, buying authority, and a measurable 14-day outcome.

## 1. North Gower Grains

**Public evidence:** North Gower / Ottawa, Eastern Ontario. The official company site was identified. GrainBids source material contains separate public Agricharts channels for North Gower corn and soybeans. An exact facility list, delivery calendar, update cadence, and current displayed fields have not yet been verified.

**GrainBids coverage and gap:** Existing Agricharts source paths provide a credible corn-and-soybean proof starting point. Coverage freshness, historical continuity, futures references, basis availability, and nearby competitor completeness remain unknown until a verified snapshot is inspected.

**Pain hypothesis:** A compact commercial team may spend recurring time comparing Ottawa Valley cash bids, basis, delivery windows, and alternative destinations.

**Tailored proof asset:** One-page Eastern Ontario corn/soybean competitive table with source-health flags and a clearly disclosed delivery-period comparison.

**Role:** Owner, General Manager, or Grain Merchandiser.

**Discovery questions:**

1. Which competing buyers and delivery windows do you check most often when setting or reviewing bids?
2. How much staff time goes into assembling those comparisons, and where are stale or missing bids most disruptive?
3. Would a weekly snapshot solve the problem, or do bid changes need business-day monitoring to be useful?

**Proposed step:** CAD $750 Competitive Market Snapshot for one Eastern Ontario region and up to two commodities, using only verified existing sources.

**Verification checklist:**

- [ ] Confirm the official facility footprint and how the company names its grain location(s).
- [ ] Recheck both public corn and soybean channels, timestamp behavior, units, and delivery labels.
- [ ] Confirm which comparison sources are currently covered and fresh in GrainBids.
- [ ] Keep any freight comparison customer-supplied and assumption-labelled.

**Official sources:** [Company site](https://www.northgowergrains.com/); [public corn bids](https://fmn1.agricharts.com/markets/cash.php?location_filter=2640); [public soybean bids](https://fmn1.agricharts.com/markets/cash.php?location_filter=2642).
